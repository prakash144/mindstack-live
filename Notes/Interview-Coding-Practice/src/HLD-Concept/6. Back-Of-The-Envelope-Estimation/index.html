    Back o Back-Of-The-Envelope Estimation¶

    Perform back-of-the-envelope estimations for designing large-scale systems like Facebook. It explains the process of estimating traffic, storage, and RAM requirements, while incorporating simplified assumptions to guide efficient system planning. The CAP theorem is also discussed, with a focus on trade-offs between consistency, availability, and partition tolerance.

    Important Before Planning¶

    • No. of Zero:
      1. Thousand (3 zeros) ‚Üí KB
      2. Million (6 zeros) ‚Üí MB
      3. Billion (9 zeros) ‚Üí GB
      4. Trillion (12 zeros) ‚Üí TB
      5. Quadrillion (15 zeros) ‚Üí PB

    Value changes every 3 zeros: - Example: 5 million users (6 zeros) * 2 KB = 10 GB of data.

    Assumptions for Estimation:¶

    • Character (ASCII): 1 Byte
    • Character (Unicode): 2 Bytes
    • Long/Double: 8 Bytes
    • Image: Average size of 300 KB

    General Calculation Strategy:¶

    1. Number of Servers: Estimate based on expected traffic and required throughput.
    2. RAM: Based on caching strategies for fast access to frequently accessed data.
    3. Storage: Estimate the storage requirement by calculating total data produced and stored over time.

    Facebook Example:¶

    1. Traffic Estimation:¶

    • Total Users: 1 Billion
    • Daily Active Users (DAU): 25% of total users ‚Üí 250 million users
    • Queries per Day: 5 read queries + 2 write queries per user = 7 queries
    • Traffic per Second:
      250 million users √ó 7 queries √∑ 60 √ó 60 √ó 24 ‚âà 18,000 queries per second

    2. Storage Estimation:¶

    • Posts: Every active user posts twice daily, with 250 characters per post.

      • 1 post: 2 √ó 250 characters = 500 bytes ‚Üí Approx 1 KB
      • Total storage for posts:
        250 million users √ó 1 KB = 250 GB/day

    • Images: 10% of active users upload images, with an average image size of 300 KB.

      • Total storage for images:
        250 million users √ó 10% √ó 300 KB = 75,000 GB/day ‚âà 8 TB/day

    • Storage Needed for 5 Years:

      • Posts:
        2,000 days √ó 250 GB/day = 500 TB
      • Images:
        2,000 days √ó 8 TB/day = 16 PB

    3. Memory (RAM) Estimation:¶

    • Cache Strategy: Cache the last 5 posts of every daily active user.
      • For 5 posts: 5 √ó 500 bytes = 2,500 bytes ‚âà 3 KB/user
      • Total RAM needed:
        250 million users √ó 3 KB = 750 GB of RAM
      • Assuming one machine can handle 75 GB, we need:
        750 GB √∑ 75 GB/machine = 10 machines for caching

    4. Latency Estimation:¶

    • Performance Expectation: 95% of requests should be served within 500 ms.
    • 1 server: Can handle 100 requests per second (50 threads, 2 requests per second).
      • Total servers needed:
        18,000 requests per second √∑ 100 requests/server = 180 servers

    Final Estimations:¶

    • Number of Servers: 180 servers to handle 18,000 queries per second.
    • RAM: 750 GB of RAM, requiring 10 cache nodes.
    • Storage:
      • Posts: 500 TB of storage for 5 years.
      • Images: 16 PB of storage for 5 years.

    Trade-offs Based on the CAP Theorem:¶

    • AP System: For a system like Facebook, prioritizing Availability and Partition Tolerance (AP) is preferable, potentially compromising on Consistency to ensure high availability and fault tolerance across distributed nodes.
