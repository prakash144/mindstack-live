    üèß Design an ATM System¶

    üõ†Ô∏è Overview¶

    An Automated Teller Machine (ATM) is an electronic telecommunications device that provides clients of a financial institution access to financial transactions in a public space without needing a cashier or bank teller.
    ATMs offer convenience as they operate even when bank branches are closed.

    ‚úÖ Requirements and Goals¶

    üî• Main Components of the ATM:¶

    • üí≥ Card Reader: Reads the users' ATM cards.
    • üî¢ Keypad: Allows users to enter information (e.g., PIN).
    • üñ•Ô∏è Screen: Displays messages and instructions.
    • üíµ Cash Dispenser: Dispenses cash to customers.
    • üí∞ Deposit Slot: Enables users to deposit cash or checks.
    • üñ®Ô∏è Printer: Prints receipts for transactions.
    • üåê Communication/Network Infrastructure:
      • Ensures the ATM communicates with the bank during transactions.

    üõ°Ô∏è Supported Transactions:¶

    • üí∞ Balance Inquiry: Check the account balance.
    • üíµ Deposit Cash: Add cash to the account.
    • üìù Deposit Check: Add check deposits (pending verification).
    • üí∏ Withdraw Cash: Withdraw money from checking accounts.
    • üîÅ Transfer Funds: Transfer money between accounts.

    üî• How ATM Works?¶

    • The ATM is managed by an operator who refills it with cash and receipts.
    • It serves one customer at a time and should not shut down mid-service.
    • Transaction Flow:
      1. Insert ATM card ‚Üí Identify user.
      2. Enter PIN ‚Üí Authenticate with the bank.
      3. Perform transactions (balance inquiry, withdrawal, deposit, etc.).
      4. Session ends ‚Üí ATM ejects the card.
    • The ATM maintains an internal transaction log for hardware failures, aiding the operator in resolving issues.

    üõ°Ô∏è Actors in the System:¶

    • üõ†Ô∏è Operator:

      • Turn ATM ON/OFF using a key-switch.
      • Refill cash and receipts.
      • Replace printer ink.
      • Remove deposited cash and checks.

    • üë§ Customer:

      • Balance Inquiry: View account balance.
      • Cash Withdrawal: Withdraw a specified amount.
      • Deposit Funds: Deposit cash or checks.
      • Transfer Funds: Transfer money between accounts.

    • üè¶ Bank Manager:

      • Generate reports for total deposits and withdrawals.
      • Print deposit/withdrawal reports.
