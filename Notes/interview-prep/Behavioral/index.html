    🧠 Behavioral & Soft Skills (STAR-based)¶

    ⭐⭐ Tell me about a time you collaborated with a Product Owner or Designer. How did you handle differing viewpoints? 🤝¶

    • 🏢 Context: Collaborated with Product Owners at CallHealth (Insurance & Blog modules) and Delhivery (FMS, Billing).
    • 🎯 Role: Responsible for aligning backend implementation with business goals.
    • 🔄 Challenge: Faced conflicts regarding technical feasibility and timeline expectations.

    • 🛠️ Action Taken:

      • Clearly explained technical constraints and trade-offs.
      • Proposed feasible alternatives with logical reasoning.
      • Used examples to demonstrate real-world impact.

      • ✅ Outcome:

      • Achieved mutual understanding.

      • Strengthened trust and collaboration across teams.

    💡 Example: In Billing, real-time bulk invoice generation was requested. I proposed batch processing instead to handle performance constraints — this was accepted.

    ⭐⭐ Describe a situation where you had to quickly learn a new tool or technology. What was the outcome? ⚡¶

    • 🏢 Context: Required to use Kafka for billing events at Delhivery.
    • 🎯 Task: Implement new Kafka event flow in a tight sprint deadline.

    • 🛠️ Action Taken:

      • Referred to internal and online Kafka resources.
      • Pair-programmed initial tasks with experienced colleagues.

      • ✅ Outcome:

      • Delivered Kafka integration in under a week.

      • Later handled other Kafka-based services confidently.

    💡 Tip: Always emphasize tool/tech name, learning approach, and business impact.

    ⭐ Have you ever received constructive feedback on your code? How did you respond, and what did you do next? 📚¶

    • 🏢 Context: Code review feedback at Delhivery.
    • 🎯 Issue: API logic too tightly coupled with DB schema.

    • 🛠️ Action Taken:

      • Used DTOs and service abstraction.
      • Applied the same pattern in future projects.

      • ✅ Outcome:

      • Improved maintainability.

      • Gained recognition to mentor juniors on service design.

    💡 Tip: Show you value feedback and act on it constructively.

    ⭐⭐ Tell us about a time when your code caused a bug in production. What did you learn from it? ⚠️¶

    • 🏢 Context: Date parsing error in FMS during quick production release.
    • 🎯 Impact: Shipment processing affected.

    • 🛠️ Action Taken:

      • Rolled back release.
      • Delivered hotfix in an hour.
      • Added automated tests and logging for date formats.

      • ✅ Outcome:

      • Improved early detection.

      • Reduced future production issues.

    💡 Tip: Own the mistake, highlight fixes and preventive measures.

    ⭐⭐ Describe how you contribute to maintaining or improving code quality in your team. 🛠️¶

    • 🏢 Context: Critical in high-scale systems like Delhivery.

    • 🛠️ Initiatives Taken:

      • Active participation in code reviews.
      • Introduced code linters and pre-commit hooks.
      • Initiated team-wide code walkthroughs.

      • ✅ Outcome:

      • Reduced bugs.

      • Faster onboarding of new developers.
      • Increased code consistency.

    💡 Tip: Mention tooling, mentoring, or process improvements.

    ⭐⭐ How do you manage competing priorities in your projects? ⏳¶

    • 🏢 Context: Multiple ongoing high-priority features.

    • 🛠️ Action Taken:

      • Close coordination with POs/PMs to prioritize.
      • Broke features into milestones.
      • Set clear expectations with stakeholders.

      • ✅ Outcome:

      • Delivered without compromising on quality.

      • Improved delivery predictability.

    💡 Tip: Showcase communication and time management skills.

    ⭐⭐ Tell me about a time when you had to deal with a difficult stakeholder. How did you handle the situation? 💬¶

    • 🏢 Context: Timeline concerns in FMS module.

    • 🛠️ Action Taken:

      • Scheduled a dedicated sync-up.
      • Shared current status, risks, and revised plan.

      • ✅ Outcome:

      • Stakeholder appreciated transparency.

      • Project delivered on time.

    💡 Tip: Be empathetic, transparent, and solution-focused.

    ⭐ How do you ensure smooth collaboration with cross-functional teams (Product, Design, etc.)? 🧑‍🤝‍🧑¶

    • 🏢 Context: Backend-Frontend-Product coordination at Delhivery.

    • 🛠️ Action Taken:

      • Regular syncs with Product, Design, QA.
      • Shared backend progress.
      • Gathered early feedback to prevent rework.

      • ✅ Outcome:

      • Timely delivery of features.

      • Alignment on user expectations.

    💡 Tip: Highlight proactive communication and stakeholder engagement.

    ⭐⭐⭐ Describe a challenge you faced while scaling a system. How did you overcome it? 🚀¶

    • 🏢 Context: Scaling Delhivery's billing system for peak season.
    • 🎯 Challenge: Performance degradation due to load.

    • 🛠️ Action Taken:

      • Identified bottlenecks.
      • Introduced caching and optimized DB queries.
      • Scaled infrastructure.

      • ✅ Outcome:

      • Smooth functioning during high load.

      • No performance-related incidents.

    💡 Tip: Name specific optimization strategies or tools used.

    ⭐⭐ What’s your approach to learning and staying updated in the fast-evolving tech landscape? 📚🌐¶

    • 🧠 Mindset: Continuous learner.
    • 📅 Routine: Weekly slots for learning.

    • 🛠️ Sources:

      • Tech blogs, newsletters (e.g., Medium, InfoQ).
      • Webinars and YouTube channels.
      • Online courses on Udemy, Coursera.

      • ✅ Impact:

      • Quickly adapt to new tech stacks.

      • Applied learnings to deliver better backend solutions.

    💡 Tip: Always link learning to practical outcomes.
